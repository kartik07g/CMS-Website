What does Phonics mean?

Phonics is a branch of linguistics where the sounds and physical properties of human speech sounds are studied. Simply put, it is a system of relationships between letters and sounds in a language. Phonics is an effective tool and a multi-sensory method to teach young children to read and spell as they are taught to link an individual letter or letter combination with its appropriate sound and then blend the sounds to form words. The goal of phonics is to enable 'beginning readers' i.e. young pre-scholarsâ€™ to decode new written words by sounding them out so that they are able to read and spell well.

All in all, phonics makes it much easier for the children to acquire, learn and master literacy skills of reading, writing and spelling. Not only these, we must understand that children who are able to communicate ably in English are bound to perform well in their academics because they can use English as a means to read more.
